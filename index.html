What is Plastic Pollution?

Plastic pollution occurs when plastic waste appears in the environment faster than it can be properly broken down. Because plastic is designed to be durable, it can take hundreds of years to decompose. During that time it often ends up in landfills, rivers, and oceans. Single-use plastics such as water bottles and straws are some of the most common contributors to this problem. When these items are thrown away, they can travel through waterways and reach the ocean, where they remain for decades.

This issue is a major environmental concern because plastic pollution harms wildlife, damages ecosystems, and affects human health. Marine animals such as turtles, birds, and fish often mistake plastic for food, which can lead to starvation or injury. Plastic also breaks down into microplastics that contaminate water and food sources. Scientists have found microplastics in oceans, soil, and even drinking water. Because of these widespread effects, plastic pollution is of the most urgent environmental challenges facing the world today.

Facts You Should Know

  • Over 8 million tons of plastic enter the ocean every year.
  • Plastic can take 400–1000 years to decompose.
  • Over 100,000 marine animals die each year from plastic waste.
  • Microplastics have been found in drinking water and seafood.
